## 1. Executive Summary

Meridian Financial Services is evaluating whether to approve the Meridian Automated Loan Underwriting System for production deployment. The system uses the third-party CrediSure Credit Decision Engine from CrediSure AI to evaluate small business loan applications and produce one of three outcomes: auto-approve, auto-deny, or route to manual review. The proposed operating model would process approximately **94% of applications automatically**, with approximately **6% routed to human review**.

Because the system affects access to credit, it is classified as a high-risk AI system. The system presents meaningful business value, but also creates material risks related to fairness, proxy bias, explainability, third-party accountability, automated denials, and human oversight. **Full production approval should not be granted at this stage.**

## 4. Business Rationale

The anticipated business benefits include:

- Reduced loan decision turnaround time
- Improved underwriting consistency
- Scaled small business lending operations
- Reduced manual review burden
- Improved applicant response speed
- More systematic portfolio monitoring

> **Governance Note:** Efficiency alone is not sufficient justification for approving a high-risk automated credit decisioning system. The system must also demonstrate that decisions are fair, explainable, appealable, monitored, auditable, and subject to meaningful human oversight.

---

## 5. High-Risk Classification Rationale

The system is classified as **High Risk** because it:

- Affects access to credit for small business applicants
- Can automatically approve or deny loan applications at scale
- Uses sensitive financial and credit-related data
- Relies on a third-party AI model with limited internal transparency
- May create discriminatory or disparate outcomes across applicant groups
- May rely on proxy variables that correlate with protected characteristics
- Requires explainable denial reason codes for adverse action compliance
- Routes only a small percentage of applications to human review

## 8. Key Governance Concerns

**8.1 Fairness and Disparate Impact**
The system may create unfair approval or denial outcomes if model inputs, training data, or decision thresholds reflect historical lending disparities. Fairness testing and segment-level monitoring must be completed before production.

**8.2 Proxy Bias**
Variables such as geography, industry, business age, credit history, thin credit files, and cash-flow volatility may act as proxies for protected or disadvantaged groups. Proxy-bias review is required before launch and after material model updates.

**8.3 Explainability and Reason Codes**
Automated denials must be supported by clear, accurate, and reviewable reason codes. Reason code validation is required before auto-denials are permitted.

**8.4 Human Oversight**
The proposed 6% manual review rate is not sufficient by itself. Human oversight must include clear review triggers, override authority, appeal handling, sampling, escalation, monitoring, and pause criteria.

**8.5 Vendor Accountability**
Meridian remains accountable for the use of the system even though the model is provided by CrediSure AI. Vendor obligations must cover documentation, validation evidence, model updates, audit support, incident notification, and security requirements.

**8.6 Security and Privacy**
The system may process sensitive financial, credit, business, and guarantor-related data. Security and privacy review must be completed before production integration.

## 9. Required Conditions Before Production

Production deployment is conditional on completion of **all** of the following:

- Complete fairness and disparate impact testing
- Complete proxy-bias review of high-risk variables
- Validate denial reason codes
- Finalize human review routing rules
- Approve the appeal and reconsideration procedure
- Confirm human reviewer override authority
- Complete vendor and model documentation review
- Confirm vendor model update notification obligations
- Complete security and privacy assessment
- Confirm decision-level audit logging
- Define monitoring thresholds
- Finalize incident escalation and pause criteria
- Complete stakeholder training
- Obtain governance committee sign-off

---

## 10. Conditions for Limited Production Pilot

A limited pilot may be considered only if Meridian can demonstrate all of the following:

- No unresolved critical fairness findings
- Reason codes are validated for adverse decisions
- Auto-denials are appealable through a documented process
- Borderline and low-confidence cases route to human review
- Human reviewers can override AI recommendations
- Audit logging is active and confirmed
- Monitoring dashboards or reports are available
- Vendor change notifications are contractually required
- Security review is complete
- Escalation and pause procedures are approved

---

## 11. Residual Risk Position

The most significant residual risks are:

- Potential undetected proxy bias
- Explainability limitations from vendor model dependency
- Model performance changes over time
- Human reviewer over-reliance on AI recommendations
- Vendor model changes affecting decision outcomes
- Applicant harm if monitoring or appeal controls fail

> **Residual Risk Acceptance:** Residual risk may be acceptable only if risk owners formally accept it in writing and monitoring confirms that controls are operating as intended.
