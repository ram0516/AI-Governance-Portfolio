## 1. Executive Summary

Meridian Financial Services is evaluating CrediSure AI as the third-party provider of the CrediSure Credit Decision Engine — an AI-enabled credit decisioning model that powers the Meridian Automated Loan Underwriting System. The model generates credit risk scores, confidence scores, approval and denial recommendations, manual review flags, and denial reason codes for small business loan applications.

Because the model may influence or directly produce credit decisions, it creates significant third-party AI governance risk. Meridian remains accountable for how the system is used in lending, even when the model is supplied by an external vendor.

**Review Status:** Vendor and model review is incomplete. Production approval must not proceed until required vendor documentation, validation evidence, security evidence, model change obligations, and monitoring commitments are reviewed and accepted.

## 5. Known Model Limitations

| **Limitation** | **Detail** |
|---|---|
| **Limited Transparency into Training Data** | Meridian does not yet have full visibility into the model's training data sources, representativeness, exclusions, or historical bias controls |
| **Limited Visibility into Model Architecture** | Meridian does not yet have sufficient information about model design, feature weighting, performance boundaries, or explainability methodology |
| **Reason Code Dependency** | Meridian depends on vendor-generated reason codes to support adverse action explanations, appeals, and compliance review. Accuracy and adequacy has not been independently validated |
| **Model Update Dependency** | Vendor model updates could affect scoring, approval rates, denial rates, fairness outcomes, or reason code behavior without advance notice to Meridian |
| **Potential Performance Drift** | Model performance may degrade due to economic shifts, portfolio changes, borrower behavior changes, or changes in credit conditions |
| **Limited Independent Validation** | Meridian has not yet completed independent validation of the model's fairness, accuracy, stability, explainability, and monitoring thresholds |
