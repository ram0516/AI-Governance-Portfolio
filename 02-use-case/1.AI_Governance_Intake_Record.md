## 1. Executive Summary

Meridian Financial Services is evaluating the Meridian Automated Loan Underwriting System, an AI-enabled credit decisioning solution designed to support small business lending. The system uses a third-party AI model — the CrediSure Credit Decision Engine, provided by CrediSure AI — to evaluate loan applications and generate one of three outcomes: auto-approve, auto-deny, or route to manual review.

The proposed operating model would allow approximately **94% of loan applications** to be processed automatically, with approximately **6% routed to human underwriting review**. Because this system materially affects access to credit, it is classified as a high-risk AI system requiring formal governance review before production deployment.

**Governance Recommendation:** Conditionally approve limited production deployment only after required controls, evidence, monitoring, human oversight, appeal procedures, and governance approvals are completed.

## 3. Business Purpose

The system is intended to improve the speed, consistency, and scalability of small business loan decisions at Meridian Financial Services.

- Reduce manual underwriting time and processing costs
- Improve consistency and repeatability in loan decisioning
- Support faster applicant response times
- Increase operational efficiency across the small business lending portfolio
- Route higher-risk or uncertain applications to qualified human underwriters
- Support credit risk assessment using structured financial and credit data

---

## 4. Intended Use

The system is approved to evaluate small business loan applications and generate underwriting recommendations or automated decisions. Permitted uses include:

- Assess small business credit risk using structured financial and credit data
- Score loan applications and generate risk and confidence scores
- Recommend approval or denial with supporting reason codes
- Automatically approve qualifying applications that meet defined risk thresholds
- Automatically deny applications that fail defined risk thresholds
- Route selected applications to manual underwriting review
- Generate denial reason codes to support adverse action communication
- Support portfolio monitoring and governance reporting

---

## 5. Out-of-Scope Use

The system must not be used for the following purposes. Any expansion beyond small business loan underwriting requires a new AI governance review.

- Consumer mortgage lending, personal loans, or student loans
- Employment screening or insurance eligibility determinations
- Healthcare eligibility or criminal justice decisions
- Fully autonomous production use without governance approval
- Credit decisions without appeal or reconsideration procedures
- Credit decisions where the denial reason code cannot be explained or validated

## 8. AI System Workflow

| **Step** | **Action** |
|---|---|
| **Step 1 — Application Intake** | Applicant submits a small business loan application through Meridian's lending platform |
| **Step 2 — Data Collection** | Meridian collects application, financial, credit, banking, and fraud-screening data |
| **Step 3 — Model Processing** | Data is transmitted to the CrediSure Credit Decision Engine via API for scoring and evaluation |
| **Step 4 — Output Generation** | The model generates a risk score, confidence score, decision recommendation, and reason codes |
| **Step 5 — Decision Routing** | The system produces one of three outcomes: Auto-approve, Auto-deny, or Manual review |
| **Step 6 — Human Review** | Manual review cases are routed to qualified human underwriters for independent assessment |
| **Step 7 — Decision Recording** | Approved or denied decisions are recorded with supporting reason codes and audit trail |
| **Step 8 — Adverse Action** | Denial reason codes are used to support adverse action communication and applicant appeal rights |
| **Step 9 — Monitoring** | Results are monitored for fairness, accuracy, drift, complaints, appeals, and performance changes |

## 13. High-Risk Classification Rationale

This system is classified as **High Risk** based on the following factors:

- Affects access to credit — a consequential financial outcome for applicants
- Produces automated approval and denial outcomes at scale
- Uses sensitive financial and credit-related personal data
- May create discriminatory or disparate impacts on protected groups
- May rely on proxy variables that correlate with protected characteristics
- Requires explainable denial reasons to support adverse action obligations
- Depends on a third-party AI model with limited internal visibility
- Has limited manual review coverage (6%) relative to automation volume

## 15. Key Risks Identified

| **Risk** | **Description** |
|---|---|
| **Discriminatory Lending Outcomes** | Automated decisions may create unfair approval or denial outcomes for protected or vulnerable groups |
| **Proxy Bias Through Credit and Business Variables** | Variables such as geography, business age, industry, credit history, and cash-flow volatility may act as proxies for protected or disadvantaged groups |
| **Weak Explainability and Incomplete Reason Codes** | Denial reasons may be too generic, inaccurate, incomplete, or difficult to validate, undermining adverse action obligations |
| **Accountability Gaps in Third-Party AI Deployment** | Responsibilities may be unclear between Meridian, CrediSure AI, and the review teams |
| **Inaccurate Automated Denials** | Qualified applicants may be incorrectly denied due to model error, data quality issues, or strict thresholds |
| **Lack of Meaningful Human Oversight** | The system may route too few applications to human review, making oversight symbolic rather than substantive |

---

## 16. Required Controls

The following controls must be implemented and documented before production deployment is approved:

- Fairness and disparate impact testing across applicant segments
- Proxy-bias review for variables correlated with protected characteristics
- Data quality validation for all model input fields
- Reason code validation to confirm accuracy and adequacy for adverse action
- Human review routing rules with defined triggers and escalation criteria
- Appeal and reconsideration procedure for applicants who receive automated denials
- Manual override authority for human underwriters
- Decision-level audit logging for all automated and manual outcomes
- Vendor due diligence covering data handling, model validation, and contractual obligations
- Monitoring thresholds for approval rates, denial rates, fairness metrics, and model drift
- Governance committee approval before production launch

## 17. Initial Governance Recommendation

**Recommendation:** Conditionally approve limited production deployment only after required controls and evidence are completed.

The system should not be fully approved for production until Meridian can demonstrate that automated decisions are fair, explainable, appealable, monitored, and auditable.
